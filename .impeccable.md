## Design Context

### Users
Solo user. Uses the app in quiet moments — typically morning coffee — to reflect on their personal relationships and decide who to reach out to. Not a sales CRM. Not a team tool. Not used while alt-tabbing against Slack during the workday. The emotional job: helping a thoughtful person stay present with the people they care about without feeling like they're using productivity software.

### Brand Personality
**Warm, intentional, quiet — with controlled pops of color.**

Voice analogues: a well-kept leather journal. A handwritten note on a Saturday morning. A specialty coffee brand's packaging where every ingredient is deliberate. An independent bookshop's in-house newsletter. NOT: enterprise SaaS, Discord's dark UI, sales CRMs, productivity dashboards, neon dark-mode aesthetics.

Emotional targets: calm, presence, permission to move slowly. Never urgency or FOMO.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Discord's *structure*, not its *aesthetic***. Chat is the primary surface — every person is a conversation. But the visual world is a warm journal, not a gaming app.
- **Light, warm paper canvas.** Cream/paper backgrounds with OKLCH-tuned neutrals (all tinted toward warm hues, ~55–70 on the color wheel).
- **Terracotta / burnt-orange primary accent** — morning-coffee warm, not the moss green of the initial sketch.
- **Typography with character**:
  - Display: **Young Serif** (Google Fonts) — warm, slab-ish, distinctive.
  - Body: **Hanken Grotesk** (Google Fonts, variable) — quiet humanist grotesk.
  - No DM Sans, Inter, or any other default reflex.
- **Line icons** (lucide-svelte) throughout the chrome — never emoji. Emoji is reserved for user-generated content (messages, notes) where a person typed it themselves.
- **Color as punctuation, not decoration.** Source chips and reminders are the intentional pops against a quiet canvas.

### Design Principles
1. **Chat is the surface.** Lists, profiles, reminders all orbit around the conversation with a specific person. Never lead with a dashboard.
2. **Warmth over sterility.** Every neutral is tinted. No pure black, no pure white, no cool gray. If it looks like a hospital form, undo it.
3. **Color is earned.** 60% warm neutral / 30% secondary / 10% accent. Source chips are loud *because* the rest of the UI is quiet.
4. **Typography carries the voice.** Use the serif display face for names, dates, and moments that deserve weight. Let body text be clean and unfussy.
5. **Specific over generic.** No generic SaaS chrome. Every detail should feel considered — not "what does a CRM look like?" but "what does *this* CRM feel like in the hands of this one person on a Saturday morning?"
